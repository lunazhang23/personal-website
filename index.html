The Shift: From Personal Distress to Systemic Support

During my undergraduate studies in Architecture, I realized that what I interpreted as personal inadequacy was often a systemic failure of the learning environment. This realization sparked my journey.

Now, as an IELTS educator and researcher, I apply the rigors of architectural design to education. I don't just teach; I design interventions. My goal is to make the "invisible" cognitive process visible.

"Effective teaching extends far beyond the charisma of an individual teacher; it is a science that can be analyzed, optimized, and improved."
