01. Theoretical Basis

Attribution Therapy Revisited

Based on Wilson & Linville (1982)

Many college freshmen interpret poor academic performance as a sign of low ability (a stable, internal cause). This mindset leads to a "fog" of helplessness and drop-out risks.

"The intervention goal is to shift attributions from stable causes (I am stupid) to unstable causes (I need better methods)."

My design applies this psychological mechanism into a gamified narrative, helping students realize that low grades are temporary and modifiable.
