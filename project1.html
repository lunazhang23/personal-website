RESEARCH • 2023-2024

Comparative Dialogue Analysis via ICAP

Overview

This independent research project focused on coding classroom interactions to identify the differences between novice and expert teachers. By utilizing the ICAP framework (Interactive, Constructive, Active, Passive), I aimed to make the "invisible" scaffolding strategies visible.

Methodology

I employed a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative coding of 10+ hours of classroom transcripts with qualitative discourse analysis. This allowed me to map out the frequency and depth of cognitive engagement prompted by teachers.

Key Findings

The data revealed that expert teachers used significantly more "Constructive" prompts that encouraged students to generate new ideas, whereas novice teachers relied heavily on "Passive" lecture styles.
